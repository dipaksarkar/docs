# Setting up your payments

You can manage your payment methods to receive payments for subscriptions and online sales within the Mailzor platform.

## Setup Stripe
Millions of businesses of all sizes—from startups to large enterprises—use Stripe’s software and APIs to accept payments, send payouts, and manage their businesses online.

- **Publishable Key**: The public key provided by Stripe for client-side tokenization.
- **Secret Key**: The private key used for server-side API communication.
- **Webhook Secret**: Secret key used to verify that incoming webhooks are genuinely from Stripe.
- **Webhook Endpoint**: The URL where Stripe will send event notifications.
  - Example: `https://your-domain.com/stripe/webhook`
- **Active**: Toggle to enable or disable Stripe as a payment option.
- **Test Mode**: Enable this to process transactions using Stripe's sandbox environment.

## Webhook Events

Stripe sends various webhook events to notify Mailzor about transaction updates. Ensure your Stripe dashboard is configured to send the following events:

### Invoice Events
- `invoice.payment_succeeded`: Confirms a subscription renewal or initial payment.
- `invoice.payment_action_required`: Indicates that 3D Secure or other verification is needed.

### Payment Intent Events
- `payment_intent.succeeded`: Confirms a successful checkout session.
- `payment_intent.requires_action`: Indicates additional customer authentication is required.

### Subscription Events
- `customer.subscription.created`: Triggered when a new user signs up for a plan.
- `customer.subscription.updated`: Triggered when a user upgrades or changes their plan.
- `customer.subscription.deleted`: Triggered when a subscription is canceled or expires.

For more details, refer to the [Stripe Webhook Documentation](https://stripe.com/docs/webhooks).

**Steps to Configure:**
1.  From your Mailzor admin, go to **Settings > Payments**.
2.  Locate **Stripe** and click the **Edit** icon.
3.  Enter your **Publishable Key**, **Secret Key**, and **Webhook Secret**.
4.  Click **Done**.

## Setup PayPal

PayPal is a global leader in online payments, offering customers a familiar and secure checkout experience via PayPal accounts or credit cards.

### Required Credentials
- **Client ID**: Unique identifier for your PayPal REST API application.
- **Client Secret**: Private key for PayPal API authentication.
- **Webhook ID**: The unique ID generated by PayPal after you register your webhook URL.
- **Webhook Endpoint**: The URL where PayPal will send event notifications.
  - Example: `https://your-domain.com/paypal/webhook`
- **Active**: Toggle to enable PayPal as a payment method.
- **Test Mode**: Enable "Sandbox" mode for testing transactions.

### Getting PayPal Credentials
1. Log in to the [PayPal Developer Dashboard](https://developer.paypal.com/dashboard/).
2. Go to **Apps & Credentials** and create a new app (e.g., "Mailzor Checkout").
3. Copy your **Client ID** and **Secret**.
4. Under the **Webhooks** section, click **Add Webhook** and enter your endpoint URL.
5. Select the relevant events (e.g., `BILLING.SUBSCRIPTION.*`, `PAYMENT.CAPTURE.*`) and save to get your **Webhook ID**.

**Steps to Configure:**
1. From your admin, go to **Settings > Payments**.
2. Click the **Edit** icon for **PayPal**.
3. Enter your **Client ID**, **Client Secret**, and **Webhook ID**.
4. Click **Done**.

## Setup GoCardless

GoCardless specialized in Direct Debit payments, making it ideal for high-retention recurring subscriptions with lower transaction fees.

### Required Credentials
- **Access Token**: The API token generated in your GoCardless dashboard.
- **Webhook Secret**: Private key for webhook signature verification.
- **Webhook Endpoint**: `https://your-domain.com/gocardless/webhook`

**Steps to Configure:**
1. Log in to your [GoCardless Dashboard](https://gocardless.com/).
2. Navigate to **Developers > API keys** and generate a new Access Token.
3. Under **Webhooks**, create an endpoint with your Mailzor webhook URL.
4. Copy the **Access Token** and **Webhook Secret** into the Mailzor payment settings.
5. Click **Done**.

## Setup Razorpay

Razorpay is the leading payment gateway for the Indian market, supporting UPI, credit cards, and popular net banking options.

### Required Credentials
- **Key ID**: Your public Razorpay API key.
- **Key Secret**: Your private Razorpay API secret.
- **Webhook Secret**: The secret string you define in Razorpay to sign webhook payloads.
- **Webhook Endpoint**: `https://your-domain.com/razorpay/webhook`

**Steps to Configure:**
1. Log in to your [Razorpay Dashboard](https://razorpay.com/).
2. Go to **Settings > API Keys** to generate your Key ID and Secret.
3. Navigate to **Settings > Webhooks** and add your Mailzor webhook URL.
4. Define a **Webhook Secret** and select the subscription and payment events.
5. Enter these credentials into Mailzor and click **Done**.

## Setup Alipay

Alipay is a primary digital wallet for the Chinese market and international users.

### Required Credentials
- **APP ID**: Your unique Alipay application ID.
- **Ali Public Key**: The public key provided by Alipay.
- **Private Key**: Your application's private key.

**Steps to Configure:**
1. Log in to the [Alipay Open Platform](https://www.alipay.com/).
2. Create and configure your application to get the **APP ID**.
3. Exchange your CSR for an **Ali Public Key**.
4. Enter your **APP ID**, **Public Key**, and **Private Key** into Mailzor.
5. Click **Done**.

## Wallet Balance

Mailzor includes a built-in **Wallet** system that allows users to maintain a balance and pay for subscriptions or credits instantly.

- **Description**: Fast, secure, and instant payment from the user's account balance.
- **Configuration**: No external API keys are required. Simply toggle the **Active** status to enable this option for your users.

## Bank Transfer (Manual)

For users who prefer traditional banking, you can enable manual bank transfers.

- **Instructions**: You can customize the instructions displayed to users during checkout (e.g., Bank Name, Account Number, SWIFT code).
- **Processing**: Orders paid via bank transfer remain in **Pending** status until an administrator manually verifies the transaction and marks the order as paid in the admin panel.

## Managing Payment Status

### Deactivating a Provider
If you need to temporarily stop accepting payments via a specific gateway:
1. Navigate to **Settings > Payments**.
2. Click the provider you wish to disable.
3. Uncheck the **Active** box.
4. Click **Done**.

### Reactivating a Provider
To resume service, simply check the **Active** box again and ensure your API keys are still valid in the provider's dashboard.
