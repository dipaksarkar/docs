# Account Security

Protect your administrative account with password management, Two-Factor Authentication (2FA), and profile control.

## Profile Information

Keep your administrative identity up to date.

- **Name**: Your display name within the admin panel.
- **Email**: Your primary login email address. Changing this will require re-verification.
- **Address**: (Optional) Your personal or office address for administrative records.

## Password Management

We recommend updating your password periodically and using a strong, unique combination of characters.

1.  Click on your **Profile Avatar** in the top-right corner of the header.
2.  Select **Security** (or **Profile Settings > Security**).
3.  Enter your **Current Password**.
4.  Enter and confirm your **New Password**.
5.  Click **Update Password**.


## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Add an extra layer of security to your account by requiring a code from your mobile device during login.

### Enabling 2FA

1.  In the **Security** settings, locate the **Two Factor Authentication** section.
2.  Click **Enable**.
3.  Scan the provided QR code using an authenticator app (e.g., Google Authenticator, Authy, or 1Password).
4.  Enter the verification code from your app to confirm the setup.

### Recovery Codes

Once 2FA is enabled, Mailzor will generate several **Recovery Codes**. 

::: warning
Store these codes in a secure location (e.g., a password manager). They are the only way to access your account if you lose access to your mobile device.
:::


## Account Deletion

If you no longer need access to the Mailzor admin panel, you can delete your profile.

::: danger
Deleting your profile is permanent and will immediately revoke all your administrative privileges. If you are the only administrator, ensure you have promoted another user before performing this action.
:::

1.  Scroll to the **Delete Account** section.
2.  Enter your password to confirm your identity.
3.  Click **Delete Account**.
